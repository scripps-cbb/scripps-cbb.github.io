<div class="faq">

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Who can participate?</summary>

The hackathon is open to Scripps Research faculty, postdocs, students,
and staff, plus invited collaborators. Software engineers, ML
practitioners, and lab scientists are equally welcome — projects are
scoped so that wet-lab and computational backgrounds can both contribute.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Do I need to come with a team?</summary>

No. Day 1 starts with project pitches followed by team formation — pitch
your own idea or join one that's already on the board.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Is there a registration fee?</summary>

No. Registration is free for eligible participants.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>What should I bring?</summary>

A laptop, your charger, and any specialty adapters you need. Power strips
will be available at every team table.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Will food be provided?</summary>

Yes. Lunch is provided on both days, and dinner is provided on Day 1 for
hackathon participants. Let us know any dietary needs on the registration
form.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Can I attend remotely?</summary>

The hackathon is in-person at Scripps Research, La Jolla. Project teams
can include remote collaborators, but at least one team member should
be on site.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Will there be GPUs and compute available?</summary>

Yes. Amazon Web Services is providing cloud compute, including GPU
instances — the AWS 101 session on Day 1 walks through getting set up.
The Scripps HPC environment is also available; both are covered in the
Day 1 morning sessions.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Will I have access to Claude during the event?</summary>

Yes. Claude (including Claude Code) access will be provided. The Day 1
Agentic AI bootcamp walks through Claude Code setup, example use cases,
limitations, and how to plug into the Scripps HPC skill.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Who owns the code we write?</summary>

Teams retain ownership of what they build. We encourage open-source
releases under permissive licenses, but the choice is up to each team.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>Will there be prizes?</summary>

Yes — final project presentations on Day 2 are followed by judge
deliberations and prize announcements at lunchtime.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>What happens after the hackathon?</summary>

Each team's project gets a permanent page on this site documenting goals,
methods, and outcomes. Teams are encouraged to keep going beyond the event.

</details>

<details class="faq-item" markdown="1">
<summary>What's the code of conduct?</summary>

Be respectful, be inclusive, and contribute to a space where everyone
can do their best work. Issues can be reported to any organizer or by
email to
[{{ site.event.contact_email }}](mailto:{{ site.event.contact_email }}).

</details>

</div>
